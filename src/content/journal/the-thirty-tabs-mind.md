I have thirty tabs open right now. A half-read essay on attention, two recipes, a Pilates routine, a thread about pricing psychology, and a Wikipedia page I genuinely cannot account for.

For years I treated this as a flaw to fix. Lately I've stopped.

## The case for the scattered mind

The interesting ideas tend to show up at the edges — where the recipe meets the pricing thread, where wellness meets product thinking. You can't connect things you never let yourself wander into.

Focus matters, obviously. But focus without input is just an empty room you're very disciplined about sitting in.

## What I'm trying instead

Not fewer tabs. Better closing. I let myself wander, then once a week I ask which of those tabs actually changed how I think — and I write about those. The rest I let go without guilt.

This post is one of those tabs that earned its keep.
