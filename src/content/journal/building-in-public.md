I've been a product manager for a few years now, and lately I've been pulled toward a different question: what would it take to move into marketing?

The honest answer is that I don't have a marketing title yet. What I have is a hypothesis — that the instincts I've built as a PM (obsessing over the customer, running small experiments, letting numbers settle arguments) transfer more cleanly than the job titles suggest.

So instead of writing that down and hoping someone believes me, I'm going to test it in the open.

## The plan

This site is the experiment. I'll write the things I actually care about — work, wellness, books, the occasional life reflection — and I'll treat growing it as a marketing project I document in full.

> The portfolio isn't a separate thing I build on the side. The growth *is* the portfolio.

Every month I'll publish the real numbers in the Growth Lab: where traffic came from, what converted, what I spent on ads and what it returned. The wins and the flat months both.

## Why public

Two reasons. First, it keeps me honest — it's harder to quietly abandon something you've told people you're doing. Second, "she grew an audience from zero and showed her work" is a more interesting story than "she made a portfolio."

If you're figuring out your own transition, I hope watching mine is useful. Let's see how it goes.
