For one month I made a single rule: no email, no Slack, nothing work-shaped before 9am.

It sounds trivial. It was not.

## The before

My old mornings started with a flinch. I'd reach for my phone, absorb whatever was waiting, and arrive at my desk already reactive — answering other people's priorities before I'd named my own.

## The change

The first three days were genuinely uncomfortable; the pull to check is stronger than I wanted to admit. By the second week, mornings felt like mine again. I read, I did Pilates, I actually tasted breakfast.

The work didn't suffer. If anything I came to it sharper, because I chose what to do first instead of letting the inbox choose for me.

## The takeaway

Energy management beats time management. The same hour feels completely different depending on whether you spend it on offense or defense. I'm keeping this one.
